Three years buildingacross the MWS frontend.

MWS is the marketplace for authenticated match-worn shirts and memorabilia. For three years I've worked across its frontend: the consumer platform, the tooling behind it, and the app. A few standout features are mine end to end.

MWS marketplace

Nuxt 3 · Vue 3 · TypeScript

The consumer marketplace where collectors bid on authenticated shirts: auctions, bidding, checkout. Plus the internal admin that runs the catalog and auctions behind it.

  • Auction, bidding, and checkout flows used by collectors worldwide
  • Data-dense admin interfaces for catalog and auction management
  • Shared design-system and component work across the Nuxt app
  • Performance and accessibility passes on high-traffic pages
  • Nuxt 3

03Thesis

Projection-Based Interaction using Tangible Objects

BSc Computer Science · 2024 · University of Amsterdam

My bachelor thesis: a modular Mixed Reality framework, built in Unity, that turns any surface into an interactive space. A camera and projector track real, tangible objects and give them a digital double. The calibration, object-initialisation, and object-detection parts are all reusable, and I demonstrated them with a live RGB colour-mixing app.

Read the thesis (PDF)↗Framework on GitHub↗
What it does
  • Modular MR framework: calibration, object init, and detection as reusable parts
  • Camera + projector pipeline that tracks tangible objects in real time
  • Demonstrated with an interactive RGB colour-mixing application
  • Mixed Reality
  • Unity
  • C#
  • Computer Vision
04AI & Agentic

One layer up —building the tools other developers build with.

Most of my time now goes into agentic engineering — designing the architecture and guardrails, then authoring the skills, hooks, and multi-agent workflows that make day-to-day development faster. Mostly in Claude Code and MCP.

Harness engineering

The part I care about most. An agent is only as good as the harness around it, so I build that harness: the context it reads first, the skills it can run, the reviewers that check its work, and the hooks that fire on their own. Get it right and agentic development stays reliable instead of turning into a mess.

  • The standards and structure the rest of the team builds on
  • Guardrails that keep agent output safe and reviewable
  • Harness
